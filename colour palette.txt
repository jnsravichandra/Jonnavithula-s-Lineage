Based on the logo, here are color palettes suitable for both light and dark modes, with explanations for each:
Light Mode Palette
This palette draws on the warmer, earthy tones from the logo, with the greens providing a fresh contrast.
Primary Background (Light): #F8F4ED (A very light, creamy off-white)
Secondary Background (Subtle Contrast): #E7E1D8 (A slightly darker, warm beige)
Primary Accent (Dark Green): #3A5C4F (The deep green from the logo, good for headings, primary buttons)
Secondary Accent (Golden Brown): #8C6B3B (The rich brown/gold from the logo, good for highlights, important links)
Text (Dark for readability): #2C3E50 (A dark charcoal grey, softer than pure black)
Subtle Text/Borders: #A19A8C (A muted grey-brown, good for borders, secondary text)
Highlight (Light Green/Leaf): #8CB380 (A brighter green from the leaves, good for subtle interactive elements or icons)



Dark Mode Palette
This palette uses the deeper greens and browns as primary backgrounds, allowing the lighter accents and text to stand out elegantly.
Primary Background (Dark Green): #2A3F38 (A very dark, muted green, slightly lighter than the logo's deep green)
Secondary Background (Dark Brown/Grey): #3A3A3A (A deep, neutral charcoal that complements the green)
Primary Accent (Golden Brown): #C8A461 (A slightly brighter, more vibrant gold-brown from the logo, for important elements)
Secondary Accent (Softer Green): #5E8472 (A muted, slightly lighter green for contrast and secondary calls to action)
Text (Light for readability): #E0E0E0 (A soft white/light grey, easy on the eyes against dark backgrounds)
Subtle Text/Borders: #8A9A9A (A cool grey-green, good for dividers, secondary information)
Highlight (Bright Green/Leaf): #A9D19E (A desaturated, lighter green for interactive elements or status indicators)
